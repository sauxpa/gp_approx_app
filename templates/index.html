GP approximation of continuous functions on a compact interval

Given $n$ observations $y_1, \dots, y_n$ of a continuous function $f$ at $x_1, \dots, x_n$, the GP regression formula with kernel $k$ is: $${\hat{f}(x) = \sum_{i=1}^n \alpha_i k(x_i, x)}$$ with $\alpha = (K+\sigma_{noise}^2I)^{-1}y$, $K=(k(x_i,x_j))_{i,j=1, \dots, n}$. Below we use the Gaussian kernel : $${k(x,x') = e^{-\frac{(x-x')^2}{2\sigma_{scale}^2}}.}$$
